d_i = \max \{ a_j : 1 \leq j \leq i\} - \min \{ a_j : i \leq j \leq n\}

d = \max \{d_i : 1 \leq i \leq n\}.

\item  IMO 3

called its \emph{size}.

Given that, in this competition, the largest size of a clique is even, prove that the

(b) Prove that equality holds above for infinitely many triples of rational

Let $N$ be the number of such sequences consisting of $k$ steps and resulting

Let $M$ be the number of such sequences consisting of $k$ steps, resulting

\begin{enumerate}

\item  IMO 1
Let $n$ be a positive integer and let $a_1,\ldots,a_k$ ($k\geq 2$) be distinct integers in the set $\{1,\ldots,n\}$ such that $n$ divides $a_i(a_{i+1}-1)$ for $i=1,\ldots,k-1$.

Prove that the sequence $s_1, s_2, s_3, \dots$ is itself an arithmetic progression.

Find all possible values of $\angle CAB$.
